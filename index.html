50 Years in the Making

The material
behind the work.

Fifty years ago, Drytac began with a simple realization: the material behind the work could be just as important as the work itself.

Richard Kelley was making table mats, coasters, and plaques when a local picture framer asked to buy a roll of the dry mounting tissue he was using. One roll became two — and the numbers spoke for themselves. The future wasn't in table mats. It was in helping others mount, finish, and protect their graphics.

That pivot defined the business. Today, Drytac is a global manufacturer, engineering its own products and delivering reliable, high-performance solutions through adhesive science.

1976 Founded
Where the Name Came From

A name born from the craft.

Before Drytac, the business operated as Lisle Industries — a one-person operation producing table mats and plaques.

As demand for dry mounting tissue grew, it became clear the future wasn't in finished products, but in supplying the materials behind them.

The name reflects that foundation:

Dry
"Dry" from dry mounting
+
Tac
"Tac" from the tacking process
=
Drytac

Registered the next day, Drytac marked the beginning of a brand built on materials, performance, and long-term growth.

Drytac engineers a high-performance adhesive designed for extreme conditions, with application down to -4°F (-20°C). Today, this technology is used across a wide range of products and remains a benchmark for cold-weather performance in the industry.
